## ‚≠ê Free & Open Access

### Unpaywall

**URL:** [unpaywall.org](https://unpaywall.org/)

Browser extension that automatically finds free legal versions of papers.

**How it works:** When you're on a journal page, shows a green padlock if a free version exists.

### Open Access Button

**URL:** [openaccessbutton.org](https://openaccessbutton.org/)

Requests and finds open access versions of papers.

### CORE

**URL:** [core.ac.uk](https://core.ac.uk/)

270M+ open access research papers aggregated from repositories worldwide.

### Semantic Scholar

**URL:** [semanticscholar.org](https://www.semanticscholar.org/)

AI-powered academic search with free PDF links where available.

### BASE (Bielefeld Academic Search)

**URL:** [base-search.net](https://www.base-search.net/)

400M+ academic documents from institutional repositories.

### arXiv

**URL:** [arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/)

Free preprints in sciences. Often ahead of journal publication.

### PubMed Central

**URL:** [ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/)

Free full-text biomedical and health literature.

### Internet Archive Scholar

**URL:** [scholar.archive.org](https://scholar.archive.org/)

Full-text search of 25M+ scholarly papers.

## üí° Tips

### Finding Free Versions

1. **Try Unpaywall first** - Often finds legitimate free versions
2. **Search Google Scholar** - May show free versions
3. **Check author websites** - Researchers often post their own papers
4. **Look for preprints** - arXiv, medRxiv, etc.
5. **Use Sci-Hub as last resort** - For papers with no free alternative

### Getting Papers Without Breaking Laws

- Email the author directly - most are happy to share
- Request via Open Access Button
- Check if your library has interlibrary loan
- Look for institutional repository versions
