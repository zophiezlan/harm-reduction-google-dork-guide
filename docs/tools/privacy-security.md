## üí¨ Encrypted Messaging

### Signal (Gold Standard)

**URL:** [signal.org](https://signal.org/)

End-to-end encrypted messenger. Requires phone number.

**Best for:** Secure team communication, client contact.

### SimpleX

**URL:** [simplex.chat](https://simplex.chat/)

No phone number or email required. Maximum privacy.

**Best for:** Anonymous communication.

### Session

**URL:** [getsession.org](https://getsession.org/)

Decentralised, no phone required. Built on blockchain.

**Best for:** High-privacy needs.

### Briar

**URL:** [briarproject.org](https://briarproject.org/)

Works over Tor and mesh networks. For high-risk situations.

**Best for:** Extreme security needs.

## üåê Privacy-Focused Browsers

### Tor Browser

**URL:** [torproject.org](https://www.torproject.org/)

Maximum anonymity. Routes traffic through multiple servers.

**Best for:** Anonymous browsing, sensitive research.

### Mullvad Browser

**URL:** [mullvad.net/en/browser](https://mullvad.net/en/browser)

Tor Browser without the Tor network. Privacy-hardened Firefox.

**Best for:** Privacy without Tor.

### LibreWolf

**URL:** [librewolf.net](https://librewolf.net/)

Privacy-hardened Firefox fork. Good daily driver.

**Best for:** Everyday private browsing.

### Brave

**URL:** [brave.com](https://brave.com/)

Chromium-based with built-in privacy features.

**Best for:** Privacy with Chrome compatibility.

## üîí File Encryption

### VeraCrypt

**URL:** [veracrypt.fr](https://veracrypt.fr/)

Encrypt drives and create encrypted containers.

**Best for:** Protecting files on your computer.

### Cryptomator

**URL:** [cryptomator.org](https://cryptomator.org/)

Encrypt cloud storage files. Works with Dropbox, Google Drive, etc.

**Best for:** Securing cloud files.

### Dangerzone

**URL:** [dangerzone.rocks](https://dangerzone.rocks/)

Convert potentially malicious PDFs to safe files.

**Best for:** Opening suspicious documents safely.

## üí° Tips

### Tip 1: Use Different Browsers

Use one browser for personal use, another for sensitive work.

### Tip 2: Clear History Regularly

Or use private/incognito mode for sensitive searches.

### Tip 3: Consider Your Threat Model

What are you protecting against? Tailor your tools accordingly.

### Tip 4: Train Your Team

Privacy is only as strong as the weakest link.
